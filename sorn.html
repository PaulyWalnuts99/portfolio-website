Project Overview

S.O.R.N is a 3D action mech shooter and my senior workshop project. As the level designer, I focused on creating engaging, challenging, and immersive environments that support player flow and combat strategy. Drawing inspiration from Armored Core VI: Fires of Rubicon, I crafted levels that balance exploration, verticality, and pacing, highlighting both gameplay and narrative elements. The project serves as a capstone for my Video Game Design studies, demonstrating the skills and design approach I’ve developed throughout the program. The project is still in active development — our project was greenlit and awarded an additional 16 weeks to continue refining and expanding the game. S.O.R.N serves as the capstone project for my Video Game Design studies, showcasing the skills, design philosophy, and iterative approach I’ve built throughout the program.

Goals & Constraints

Project Goals:

  • Design a fully playable vertical slice/demo of our game by the end of the semester with a critical path between 3-8 minutes
  • Maintain comprehensive documentation throughout the project, including detailed Game Design Documents (GDDs) and Art Style Guides to ensure clarity and consistency across the team
  • Follow the guidance of the Producer and Creative Director, adhering to the established leadership structure to maintain organization, communication, and workflow efficiency
  • Record and report all hours worked on the project, with a minimum commitment of 15 hours per week, ensuring accountability and consistent progress toward project goals

Production Constraints:

  • Team composition requirements evolved throughout the semester, with minimum numbers for artists, programmers, and other roles increasing at certain checkpoints to ensure balanced teams
  • The proof-of-concept presentation needed to meet quality standards in order for the project to continue; insufficient presentations could prevent the game from moving forward
  • Only three out of five senior workshop projects were allowed to progress at the end of the semester, emphasizing the importance of consistent quality and teamwork
  • Balancing the required 15+ hours per week on the project alongside part-time work and other coursework presented a realistic but challenging time management scenario

Maps & Inspiration

The levels in S.O.R.N are designed to support fast-paced mech combat, inspired by Armored Core VI. Each environment accommodates a variety of weapons, mech builds, and dynamic encounters, encouraging players to experiment and adapt. Objectives and narrative elements are integrated seamlessly through directives and contracts, keeping players engaged without interrupting the flow of action. The design focuses on strategic, rewarding gameplay, ensuring every level challenges players while highlighting freedom of movement and combat options.

Directive 1 Walkthrough

The tutorial introduces players to the fundamental mechanics of S.O.R.N, guiding them through core systems such as movement, targeting, and combat engagement. Designed to be unmissable and impossible to bypass, the desert testing ground was deliberately crafted to ensure every player receives proper onboarding. The terrain layout naturally funnels the player toward the test turret—so they must engage with essential mechanics before entering the open world.

Beat 1: S.O.R.N Tutorial

The desert level expands the player’s understanding of navigation in a large, open space while still guiding them toward the main objective. Although the environment includes three POIs, the quest marker establishes a clear destination, with the first POI made intentionally unmissable as its guarding turrets draw the player into the encounter and help reinforce core controls. Once cleared, players naturally move toward the objective, where a giant laser turret introduces a high-pressure navigation challenge. The surrounding debris field is deliberately arranged as mandatory cover, forcing players to weave through it and manage line-of-sight to survive, ensuring the environment itself teaches pacing, spatial awareness, and strategic movement.

Beat 2: The Desert

The wall sequence shifts the experience from open-desert exploration to tight, controlled combat encounters. After infiltrating the facility through a narrow vent, the player enters a small storage room for a focused introduction to the Hermit—our newest enemy type—allowing them to engage it once before it reappears later in the mission. From there, the player is pushed into Sector 1, where enemy placements were deliberately arranged to create layered engagements across turrets, drones, and additional Hermits. The room’s set dressing reinforces both atmosphere and utility, providing meaningful cover and encouraging tactical movement. Two inactive trains occupy the space as well, acting as early visual foreshadowing for the larger train-based encounters that await deeper in the wall.

Beat 3: The Wall Sector 1

After clearing Sector 1, players progress through the next vent and encounter a bounce pad, introduced in a safe space to foreshadow its upcoming importance. Entering Sector 2, the room immediately expands in scale and height, using verticality to build on everything taught previously. Bounce pads now become a core traversal tool, enabling players to reach upper platforms and reposition during combat. A moving train cuts through the room as an active hazard—damaging the player on contact—and forces constant spatial awareness as enemies apply pressure from multiple elevations. This sector intentionally ramps up difficulty by combining new mechanics, increased vertical depth, and environmental danger into a more demanding, multi-layered encounter.

Beat 4: The Wall Sector 2

Sector 3 transitions from the open, vertical spaces of the previous rooms to a tighter, more linear section, creating a deliberate shift in pace and aesthetic to keep the level engaging. Darker, moodier lighting, scattered debris, and broken scaffolding establish a sense of danger, while still providing paths for traversal and cover. Enemy placement remains dense, forcing the player to navigate carefully through constricted corridors and engage threats with precision. This section emphasizes tension, strategic positioning, and mastery of the movement and combat systems introduced earlier, offering a contrasting, high-stakes experience before the level progresses toward the final objective.

Beat 5: The Wall Sector 3

Sector 4 marks the climax of the mission with a cutscene, musical shift, and darker lighting, signaling the final challenge. The player faces the Wall’s commander in a straightforward 1v1 fight, testing the skills learned throughout the level. Defeating the commander opens an escape route. The player descends downn the hatch and moves through the vent which exits out the top of Sector 1. The player can now exit through the front doot of the wall, which was earlier sealed off and guarded by the powerful laser turret out in the desert. This design reinforces a sense of interconnectivity, rewarding players by allowing them to traverse previously unreachable areas while providing a clear, satisfying conclusion to the mission.

Beat 6: The Wall Sector 4

Building The Terrain

The building phase began with the creation of the desert terrain, which was procedurally generated and refined using customizable parameters to achieve natural dunes and realistic elevation variation. Since S.O.R.N is designed for monolith-scale worlds and encounters—with the player’s mech standing 30 feet tall—our levels had to reflect this scale, ensuring sightlines, traversal, and combat felt appropriately massive. From the start, our goal was to design a level that felt expansive and open while maintaining a guided, mission-based structure—striking a balance between exploration and direction. This was my first experience developing a large-scale, open environment, presenting unique challenges in navigation flow, player readability, and visual composition across a vast setting. With the terrain established, the next step involved placing points of interest and encounters to guide the player, introduce gameplay mechanics, and enrich exploration. These are the initial renderings for our desert landscape.

Implementing Points of Interest

With the desert terrain established, the next phase focused on implementing points of interest (POI's) to guide player progression and introduce gameplay challenges. Key locations were carefully placed to balance exploration with mission objectives, ensuring the player naturally encounters essential mechanics while still feeling free to navigate the open environment. The first POI was designed to be unmissable, drawing the player in through environmental cues and turret engagement, while additional POIs offer optional exploration that reinforces the world’s scale and encourages strategic traversal. This phase allowed us to shape the desert experience, turning the raw landscape into a functional, engaging level.

Finalizing POI's and General Polish

The final phase focused on finalizing points of interest and applying general polish to the desert level. This involved confirming that all POIs were properly placed and functional, ensuring the player could naturally navigate through the environment and encounter key gameplay moments. Small adjustments were made to improve flow and cohesion, turning the completed terrain and POIs into a fully playable and engaging space.

The Wall: Sector 1 Blockout

The initial blockout of Sector 1 focused on establishing the core layout and spatial flow within the Wall, transitioning players from the open desert into an enclosed, tactical environment. At this stage, the geometry was simplified to basic volumes, with key rooms, corridors, and pathways mapped out to accommodate the player’s 30-foot-tall mech. Emphasis was placed on maintaining readability and navigation at monolith scale, ensuring that sightlines, cover opportunities, and combat spaces felt appropriately massive without overwhelming the player. This foundational blockout allowed us to quickly test movement, traversal, and core combat flow, creating a solid framework for iterative refinement in subsequent phases.

The Wall: Sector 1 Iteration Phase

Once the initial blockout was established, the iteration phase focused on refining the layout and gameplay using our modular kit. Leveraging a consistent world grid ensured that all pieces snapped together seamlessly, maintaining structural cohesion while allowing rapid adjustments. A major focus was on creating visually interesting geometry and dynamic rooms—avoiding simple boxes, sharp edges, and overly square layouts in favor of natural bends, flowing corridors, and spaces that felt organic while still functional for mech-scale traversal. Regular playtesting and team feedback guided adjustments to room shapes, pathways, and encounter placement, enhancing player flow and readability. This iterative process transformed the foundational blockout into a functional, engaging sector that balanced combat, traversal, and exploration within the Wall.

The Wall: Sector 1 Final Renderings

The final renderings phase brought Sector 1 to life, integrating all art assets, set dressing, and lighting to create a cohesive and immersive space. Enemy placements were locked in to ensure the combat flow aligned with the level’s design intent. This phase finalized the sector’s visual and gameplay presentation, turning the blockout and iterative adjustments into a polished, fully playable environment that clearly communicates scale, navigation, and mission objectives.

The Wall: Sector 2 Blockout

The initial blockout of Sector 2 focused on creating a larger, more vertical space that built upon the design principles from Sector 1. The layout was designed to introduce bounce pads, allowing players to reach new heights and engage with the environment in a controlled way. A moving train was included as a key environmental hazard, requiring the player to navigate carefully to avoid damage. At this stage, the blockout prioritized scale, player flow, and the integration of new mechanics, providing a solid foundation for iterative refinement.

The Wall: Sector 2 Iteration Phase

During the iteration phase, Sector 2 was refined to improve player flow, combat pacing, and interaction with new mechanics. Bounce pads were carefully positioned and tested to ensure safe introduction and intuitive use, while the moving train hazard was balanced to create challenge without frustrating the player. Feedback from playtesting guided adjustments to sightlines, enemy placements, and traversal paths, ensuring the sector felt engaging and taught players how to navigate vertical spaces effectively. This iterative process transformed the blockout into a more polished, functional environment, ready for final rendering and set dressing.

The Wall: Sector 2 Final Renderings

The final renderings phase brought Sector 2 to its completed state, integrating all art assets and set dressing to create a polished, immersive environment. Lighting was applied to highlight key paths and maintain clarity within the taller, more vertical space. Enemy placements and the moving train hazard were finalized to enhance the challenge, making the room feel naturally more difficult due to the combination of vertical traversal, environmental danger, and additional combatants. This stage solidified Sector 2 as a visually coherent and engaging area, fully playable while showcasing the new mechanics introduced in this part of the Wall.

The Wall: Sector 3 Blockout

The initial blockout for Sector 3 envisioned a massive vertical space filled with multiple moving trains, requiring the player to navigate complex platforming while engaging enemies on their ascent. The layout was designed to challenge traversal skills and spatial awareness, with paths winding around hazards and vertical elements emphasizing mech-scale movement. At this stage, the blockout prioritized the overall structure, spacing, and flow of encounters, establishing the framework for a high-intensity, layered combat and platforming experience before any visual detail or set dressing was applied.

The Wall: Sector 3 Iteration Phase

During the iteration phase, playtesting and team discussions revealed that the initial massive vertical design would be too ambitious given time constraints and workload. To maintain quality and ensure a polished experience, the team shifted to a smaller, tighter, and more linear layout—the version that appears in the game today. This new design preserved the challenge and tension of the sector while making it more manageable to develop. Feedback guided the placement of enemies, hazards, and traversal elements, ensuring that the tighter corridors still offered engaging encounters and maintained a sense of danger and progression through the Wall.

The Wall: Sector 3 Final Renderings

The final renderings phase focused on refining the linear path to ensure it felt unique, unpredictable, and visually engaging. This sector represents a broken-down portion of the Wall, with debris and damaged structures scattered throughout, reinforcing the sense of danger and environmental storytelling. Extensive playtesting guided the placement of enemies and hazards, while lighting was carefully applied to maintain readability and emphasize key paths. This phase transformed the blockout and iteration work into a cohesive, fully playable environment that feels tense, dynamic, and distinct from the previous sectors.

The Wall: Sector 4

Sector 4 is the final, boss-fight area of the Wall mission. The room is compact and straightforward, essentially a single enclosed space with server terminals along the back wall. Because of its simplicity, there was no traditional blockout or iteration required—the focus was on creating a functional arena for the 1v1 encounter with the Wall’s commander, providing enough space for the player to maneuver and engage effectively. Sector 4 serves as the culmination of the Wall mission, where players face the Wall’s commander in a 1v1 encounter that tests all of the skills and mechanics learned throughout the level. The compact server room may be simple in layout, but it demands strategic use of movement and combat techniques, encouraging players to utilize everything at their disposal to defeat the boss. Once the commander is defeated, the player navigates the escape route—descending through a hatch and exiting via the vent above Sector 1—providing a satisfying conclusion that reinforces the level’s connectivity and progression.

Conclusion & Learnings

The development of S.O.R.N provides a valuable opportunity to tackle a full-scale 3D level with both environmental storytelling and combat-driven design. Throughout the process, we navigate challenges ranging from procedural terrain creation to pacing multi-layered encounters, all while coordinating as a small, interdisciplinary team. Our iterative workflow emphasizes testing, feedback, and refinement, ensuring the player experience remains engaging and cohesive. As we move into the next semester, we have 16 weeks of development ahead and are welcoming nine new team members to help expand the project. Looking back, we identify both key lessons learned and areas for improvement that will guide our future work, such as:

Knowledge Gained:

  • Familiarity with Unreal Engine’s CubeGrid, landscaping, and foliage tools, allowing efficient blockout and natural, mech-scale environments
  • Understanding the importance of iterating upon existing levels to improve flow, pacing, and player engagement
  • Recognizing the value of working on the grid to ensure precise placement and seamless integration of modular assets
  • Proper use of modular kits and designing with clear meaning and purpose for every space, prop, and encounter

Areas For Improvement:

  • Designing with purpose and intention, making sure every element reinforces gameplay or narrative goals
  • Taking feedback and criticism and applying it effectively to improve the level while maintaining cohesion
  • Being more creative with level layouts and prop placement to enhance player experience
  • Communicating design thoughts and visions clearly through maps, diagrams, and reference images
