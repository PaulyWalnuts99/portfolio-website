Project Overview

I joined the team behind S.O.R.N, a 3D action mech shooter and my senior workshop project, after hearing their pitch alongside several others at the start of the semester. Inspired by Armored Core VI: Fires of Rubicon, I took on the role of level designer, crafting engaging and challenging environments. The team had full creative freedom over the project, and S.O.R.N serves as our capstone achievement in the Video Game Design major, showcasing the skills and experience we’ve developed throughout the program.

Goals & Constraints

Project Goals:

  • Design a fully playable vertical slice/demo of our game by the end of the semester with a critical path between 3-8 minutes.
  • Maintain comprehensive documentation throughout the project, including detailed Game Design Documents (GDDs) and Art Style Guides to ensure clarity and consistency across the team.
  • Follow the guidance of the Producer and Creative Director, adhering to the established leadership structure to maintain organization, communication, and workflow efficiency.
  • Record and report all hours worked on the project, with a minimum commitment of 15 hours per week, ensuring accountability and consistent progress toward project goals.

Production Constraints:

  • Team composition requirements evolved throughout the semester, with minimum numbers for artists, programmers, and other roles increasing at certain checkpoints to ensure balanced teams.
  • The proof-of-concept presentation needed to meet quality standards in order for the project to continue; insufficient presentations could prevent the game from moving forward.
  • Only three out of five senior workshop projects were allowed to progress at the end of the semester, emphasizing the importance of consistent quality and teamwork.
  • Balancing the required 15+ hours per week on the project alongside part-time work and other coursework presented a realistic but challenging time management scenario.

Concept & Inspiration

The design draws heavily from Armored Core VI, with all movement and combat structured around fast-paced mech action. Levels are crafted to support a variety of weapons, customizable mech parts, and dynamic encounters. The narrative is integrated through directives and contracts, providing players with context and objectives while maintaining the intensity of the combat. This approach ensures each level is both engaging and strategically challenging, highlighting the team’s focus on fluid, rewarding gameplay.

Level 1 Walkthrough

The tutorial introduces players to the fundamental mechanics of S.O.R.N, guiding them through core systems such as movement, targeting, and combat engagement. Set within a vast desert testing ground, the environment provides ample space for experimentation while emphasizing the game’s scale and mech mobility. Subtle visual cues and structured encounters help players gradually build confidence with traversal and weapon systems, establishing a strong foundation before advancing to more complex missions.

Section 1: Tutorial

The desert level expands upon the player’s understanding of movement and navigation within a large, open environment. Featuring natural terrain with sweeping dunes and steep canyon walls, this area encourages exploration while introducing new gameplay challenges. Players encounter turret enemies, scattered debris fields, and a key point of interest (POI) that guides them toward their main objective—the wall. Additional POIs are visible throughout the landscape, offering optional encounters that enrich the world’s sense of scale and realism, while subtly directing the player’s curiosity toward future objectives.

Section 2: Desert Area

The wall sequence shifts the tone from open exploration to tense, confined combat. The player infiltrates the massive enemy facility through a narrow vent, emerging into the first of several interconnected rooms. Here, they encounter drone enemies and moving supply trains that introduce new layers of environmental danger and spatial awareness. As a central hub for mech construction and transport, the wall’s industrial design emphasizes scale and danger, with shifting machinery and narrow combat zones that demand quick decision-making. The player’s objective dynamically updates with each cleared area, driving steady progression through the heart of the enemy stronghold.

Section 3: S.O.R.N Wall Room 1

Building upon the foundation of the first encounter, Room 2 expands the combat design by introducing verticality and layered traversal. Multiple elevation changes, platforms, and catwalks create opportunities for varied engagement—allowing players to approach enemies from above or below. This shift not only enhances spatial awareness but also reinforces the mech’s mobility and combat versatility. The layout encourages players to think strategically about positioning, using height and cover to their advantage while maintaining the fast-paced flow of combat established earlier in the level.

Section 4: S.O.R.N Wall Room 2

The final section of the level combines all the elements introduced throughout the mission—tight combat spaces, vertical traversal, and environmental hazards. Room 3 challenges the player to navigate a large, multi-tiered area filled with drones, turrets, and moving trains that demand constant awareness and precision. Players can explore both upper catwalks and lower sections of the facility, balancing risk and reward as they push toward their objective. The encounter culminates at the top of the room, where the player accesses a vent leading to the final chamber. There, they plant the explosive charge—completing their primary directive and signaling the end of the mission.

Section 5: S.O.R.N Wall Room 3

Building The Terrain

The building phase began with the creation of the desert terrain, which was procedurally generated and refined using customizable parameters to achieve natural dunes and realistic elevation variation. From the start, our goal was to design a level that felt expansive and open while maintaining a guided, mission-based structure—striking a balance between exploration and direction. This marks my first experience developing a large-scale, open-style environment, presenting unique challenges in navigation flow, player readability, and visual composition within a vast setting.

Iteration Phase

During the iteration phase, we focused on experimenting with ideas, integrating feedback, and leveraging our teammates’ strengths to align with the narrative and design goals. With these guiding principles, we gave the desert environment a comprehensive overhaul—reworking the foliage, handcrafting the dunes, and refining the overall layout. This phase allowed us to elevate the desert into a more cohesive and engaging space that supports the story and gameplay.

Current Progress

At this stage, the level has reached a cohesive and playable state, clearly reflecting our design intentions. The desert environment establishes strong visual landmarks that guide player navigation and reinforce mission objectives. Combat spaces have been refined to support player movement and line-of-sight dynamics, ensuring that encounters remain fast-paced yet readable. Environmental composition, waypoint placement, and enemy positioning have been iterated on to create a natural gameplay rhythm—balancing moments of tension and downtime. While still a work in progress, this version effectively demonstrates the core gameplay loop and environmental flow we set out to achieve.

Conclusion & Learnings

The development of S.O.R.N provided a valuable opportunity to tackle a full-scale 3D level with both environmental storytelling and combat-driven design. Throughout the process, we navigated challenges ranging from procedural terrain creation to pacing multi-layered encounters, all while coordinating as a small, interdisciplinary team. Our iterative workflow emphasized testing, feedback, and refinement, which helped ensure the player experience remained engaging and cohesive. By the end of the semester, we were proud of what we accomplished in such a short time. Looking back, we identified both key lessons learned and areas for improvement that will guide our future work, such as:

  • Documenting design decisions to maintain clear team communication
  • Gained hands-on experience with CubeGrid for modular level design and layout planning.
  • Developed stronger proficiency with Unreal Engine’s foliage and landscape tools to create environments that feel natural and immersive rather than overly geometric.
  • Learned how to craft engaging, visually interesting environments while maintaining gameplay clarity.
  • Understood the critical role of team communication, as well as the need to maintain and update documentation consistently.
  • Importance of early layout planning for smooth gameplay
  • Creating more detailed maps and blockouts early in development
