Level Goals and Overview

Bootlegger Bust is a level set in a Prohibition-era warehouse, designed to support the time period through environmental layout and visual structure. The primary goal was to create a readable warehouse space with clearly defined areas that guide player movement and support gameplay within the setting, focising on:

  • Design a warehouse that reflected a prohibition-era industrial setting while remaining readable for gameplay
  • Organize distinct areas within the warehouse to help players understand space, progression, and purpose at a glance
  • Support gameplay by ensuring room sizes and sightlines were consistent and supported player flow

Level Walkthrough

  • Goal: Introduce the level and player objective through a focused garage space that establishes tone and intent
  • Design Goal 1: Use minimal, moody lighting and a compact layout to guide player attention toward objectives and on-screen widgets
  • Design Goal 2: Design the garage as a tight but purposeful space that sets expectations for more complex areas later in the level

Section 1: Starting Garage

  • Section Goal: Establish context and immersion with the city street outside the warehouse
  • Design Goal 1: Introduce framing techniques to guide the player visually toward the warehouse entrance
  • Design Goal 2: Use surrounding streets and props to intuitively direct player movement toward the objective

Section 2: City Streets

  • Section Goal: Introduce the first combat area and support player engagement
  • Design Goal 1: Provide multiple cover points and clear sightlines for combat encounters
  • Design Goal 2: Use set dressing and props to create an authentic industrial environment without blocking navigation

Section 3: Warehouse Part 1

  • Section Goal: Expand the combat space while establishing a distinct tone and atmosphere
  • Design Goal 1: Use warm yellow-orange lighting and moody tones to create a vintage, immersive feel
  • Design Goal 2: Incorporate set dressing and props to convey a Prohibition-era hideout while maintaining clear readability and player flow

Section 4: Warehouse Part 2

  • Section Goal: Create a focused one-on-one encounter that contrasts larger combat spaces
  • Design Goal 1: Use red lighting and dramatic props to heighten tension and reinforce a confrontational tone
  • Design Goal 2: Provide a brief slowdown connected to the back storage area to let the player reset before the final stretch

Section 5: Warehouse Office

  • Section Goal: Introduce a tighter, darker, and more linear space to shift pacing
  • Design Goal 1: Use minimal lighting and dense shelving to create tension and anticipation
  • Design Goal 2: Apply breadcrumbing through lighting to intuitively guide player movement

Section 6: Storage Room

  • Section Goal: Deliver the final showdown and culminate the level’s escalation in difficulty and atmosphere
  • Design Goal 1: Incorporate a boss health bar to make this encounter feel unique and give the player clear combat feedback
  • Design Goal 2: Include a key drop mechanic to unlock the door outside the room, linking the encounter to level progression

Section 7: Final Boss Room

The Process

Research and References

  • Research & Ideation: Referenced prohibition-era photos and similar game levels to understand how to convey setting, flow, and combat spaces effectively
  • Reference Gathering: Collected images of warehouses, streets, props, lighting setups, and cinematic inspiration to inform environmental design and thematic consistency
  • Mapping & Organization: Mapped out room layouts, points of interest, and encounter flow to plan the overall player journey

Initial Blockout

The initial blockouts for Bootlegger Bust were designed to establish the warehouse layout and support player engagement, guiding movement, combat, and pacing throughout the level. Each blockout focused on:

  • Supported Player Mechanics: Created blockouts that provided spaces for combat, cover usage, and player navigation, reinforcing core gameplay flow within the warehouse
  • Provided Visual and Tactical Clarity: Arranged key architectural features, doors, and props to clearly communicate paths, cover, and engagement areas while staying consistent with Prohibition-era references
  • Contextualized Playspaces: Designed each room and corridor to feel intentional and connected to research and reference imagery, giving the warehouse a believable and purposeful layout

Design Challenges and Solutions

Problem 1: Unclear Critical Path

  • Goal: Create a warehouse environment that guides the player naturally through exploration and combat while keeping spaces interesting
  • Problem: Early layouts made it unclear where players were supposed to go, and the critical path was too tedious to follow
  • Solutions: Ditched the ladder and doors, and instead used a more linear and intuitive path design that encouraged forward movement and clear visual cues such as stairs

Problem 2: Level Was Too Short With Little Variance in Gameplay Beats

  • Goal: Create a level that provided 5–8 minutes of engaging gameplay with varied gameplay beats
  • Problem: The original layout was way too short, consisting of only three brief gameplay beats
  • Solution: Added a starting garage and city for slower, contextual buildup, plus a back storage room and final boss to increase intensity and differentiate gameplay beats

Conclusion & Learnings

Designing Bootlegger Bust provided a focused opportunity to refine level flow, player guidance, and gameplay pacing within a visually cohesive Prohibition-era setting. The key takeaways were:

Knowledge Gained:

    • Learned how to structure spaces and visual cues to naturally guide players through complex layouts
    • Gained insight into differentiating combat and exploration beats to maintain engagement and tension
    • Developed skills in framing key objectives and designing spaces that clearly communicate player intent

    Areas for Growth:

    • Improve early-stage layout planning to better anticipate pacing and player flow
    • Refine environmental storytelling without relying on explicit narrative cues
    • Experiment with more dynamic encounter design to create varied combat experiences
